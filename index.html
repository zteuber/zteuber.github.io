About Me

Hello and welcome! My name is Ziwen Teuber. I am a Senior Researcher in the Department of Behavioral and Cognitive Sciences at the University of Luxembourg, where I also hold PhD supervision rights. From our campus in Belval, I lead the FNR CORE project "Unpacking Parental Involvement in Challenging Achievement Situations" (UPICAS) as its principal investigator.

I earned my BSc and MSc degrees in Psychology at Bielefeld University in Germany between 2011 and 2016, and completed my PhD in Psychology there in 2021. During my doctoral studies, I explored school burnout among Chinese and German students, which sparked a broader interest in how young people navigate pressure, expectations, and emotional demands. I joined the University of Luxembourg in 2022. Since then, my research has taken a more family-centered direction. I am particularly fascinated by how everyday parent–child interactions influence well-being and development, and by how evidence-based tools can support families in managing the challenges they face.

I draw on frameworks from multiple psychological subdisciplines and apply rigorous methodologies to address contemporary challenges in education, family life, and society. I have multi-sectoral experience and aim to use my knowledge in science, praxis, and political communication to impact research and society. Furthermore, I actively support Open Science by preregistering all studies before data collection and openly sharing research materials with the scientific community.

I am currently co-editing the "Handbook of Educational Psychology" with Nadine Spörer (University of Potsdam) and Samuel Greiff (Technical University of Munich), to be published by Springer. The handbook is scheduled for release in autumn 2027.
